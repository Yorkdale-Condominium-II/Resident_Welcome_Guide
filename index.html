Welcome

This guide gives owners, tenants, occupants, and guests a practical summary of key building rules and procedures. It is designed for quick online reference and printing.

This is a summary, not a replacement for the governing documents. If this guide conflicts with the Condominium Act, the declaration, bylaws, rules, or a valid board policy, the higher-authority document applies. Operational procedures, contacts, fees, and hours may change.

The condominium rules define an "owner" to include another person occupying a unit with the owner's approval. Owners who lease their units must provide tenants with the declaration, bylaws, and rules. Losses, costs, or damages caused by a breach may be charged by the corporation to the unit owner, as permitted by the governing documents.

Everyday Courtesy

  • Please doRespect your neighbours' right to use and enjoy their units and the common elements.
  • Please doKeep hallways, stairwells, entrances, driveways, and other passageways clear.
  • Not permittedLeaving garbage, furniture, bicycles, boxes, or personal property in common areas.
  • Not permittedPosting signs, advertisements, or notices in or outside the building without prior written board consent.

Sources: Schedule XII Rules 2, 5, 6, 9 and 14; Declaration section 10.

Registration & Moving

Complete resident registration before moving in and reserve the service elevator before any move or extended delivery.

Tenant & Resident Registration

  • Summary of Lease or Renewal - Form 5
  • Tenant Undertaking and Acknowledgement
  • Copy of the lease
  • Resident Information Form
  • Vehicle registration, where applicable
  • Pet registration and photograph, where applicable
  • Parcel delivery waiver, if concierge parcel acceptance is requested

Service Elevator Booking

  • DepositA refundable security deposit of $500 by bank draft, payable to T.S.C.C. 2937, is required under the approved elevator agreement in effect at publication.
  • Time slotsThree-hour reservations: 9 AM-noon, noon-3 PM, 3-6 PM, or 6-9 PM. The form permits Monday-Saturday between 9 AM and 9 PM, with Tuesday slots between 3 PM and 9 PM.
  • No movesMoves are not permitted on Sundays or statutory holidays under the current form.
  • RequiredRequest inspections before and after the move, use protective elevator pads, stay within the reservation period, and keep corridors and elevator lobbies clear.

Source: Elevator Reservation Agreement & Inspection Checklist. Confirm the current deposit and schedule with management before booking.

Parcel Acceptance

  • WaiverConcierge parcel acceptance requires a signed waiver on file.
  • LimitsThe approved parcel form states that packages over 18 x 18 x 24 inches or 25 pounds will not be accepted. Appliances and hazardous materials are not accepted.
  • PickupCollect deliveries promptly. The form permits disposal or return to sender after seven days; perishable food is held for 24 hours.

Source: Parcel Delivery Waiver Agreement Form.

General Rules

These rules protect life safety, building systems, common areas, and neighbouring residents.

Common Elements & Safety

  • Not permittedObstructing sidewalks, entrances, passageways, walkways, driveways, hallways, stairwells, or fire exits.
  • Not permittedDoing anything in a unit that increases fire risk, conflicts with fire regulations or insurance, or injures or unreasonably interferes with others.
  • Not permittedDamaging, altering, or littering grass, trees, shrubs, hedges, flowers, or flower beds.

Water & Plumbing

  • Not permittedLeaving water running when it is not in actual use.
  • Not permittedPutting sweepings, garbage, rubbish, rags, ashes, or other substances into toilets, sinks, bathtubs, showers, or drains.
  • ResponsibilityDamage from misuse or unreasonable use may be charged to the unit owner responsible for the person who caused it.

Balconies, Patios & Terraces

  • PermittedSeasonal furniture, while keeping the area clean and presentable.
  • Not permittedBarbecuing or cooking.
  • Not permittedStoring bicycles or other items.
  • Not permittedAwnings, shades, enclosures, satellite dishes, antennas, or similar structures unless expressly permitted by the governing documents.

Sources: Schedule XII Rules 1-4, 9, 12-16.

Waste Disposal

Use the current posted waste-system instructions.

Use the chute or tri-sorter only as labelled. Separate garbage, recycling, and organics according to current building signage and management notices. Do not place hazardous materials, oversized items, furniture, or liquids in the chute.
  • Please doBag waste securely and use the correct stream shown on the chute or waste-room signage.
  • Please doAsk management for current instructions before disposing of furniture, mattresses, appliances, renovation debris, chemicals, batteries, electronics, or other oversized or hazardous items.
  • Not permittedLeaving bags, boxes, furniture, or other items in the chute room, hallway, stairwell, or other common element.
  • Not permittedPutting liquids, hazardous substances, oversized objects, or loose material into a chute where they may leak, jam, or cause damage.

Sources: Schedule XII Rules 1 and 5.

Noise & Quiet Enjoyment

Noise restrictions apply at all times, not only during a stated quiet period.

  • Not permittedCreating or continuing noise or another nuisance that, in the opinion of the board or manager, disturbs the comfort or quiet enjoyment of units or common elements.
  • Not permittedNoise from an instrument, device, party, renovation, or other source that may disturb other residents.
  • Please doKeep voices, music, door closing, moving activity, and loading noise low in hallways, elevators, lobbies, and other shared spaces.
  • Please doConfirm any current renovation hours or quiet-period policy with management before scheduling noisy work.
Noise rules apply at all times: Schedule XII Rules 6 and 8 prohibit noise or nuisance that disturbs other residents. Follow any additional hours stated in a current board policy or management notice.

Sources: Schedule XII Rules 6 and 8.

Pets

The rules permit pets subject to a two-pet maximum, control in common areas, cleanup, and nuisance restrictions.

  • MaximumUp to two pets per unit.
  • Not permittedAnimals, livestock, or fowl other than a pet.
  • LeashKeep pets on a leash in common-element areas normally travelled by pedestrians.
  • CleanupPrevent pets from defecating on common elements. If an accident occurs, clean it immediately.
  • NuisanceA pet deemed a nuisance by the board or manager, in the discretion stated in Rule 7, may not be kept on the property.
  • Removal noticeIf an owner fails to clean up after a pet, Rule 7 deems the pet a nuisance and requires permanent removal within two weeks after the owner receives written notice requesting removal.

Pet Registration

Residents shall register their pets with property management by providing a photograph, along with each pet's name, type, breed, colour, size, weight, and service animal status.

Sources: Schedule XII Rule 7; Pet Registration Form.

Parking & Vehicles

Park only in a space assigned to you or otherwise authorized. Register vehicles with management.

  • Permitted typesMotorcycles, private passenger automobiles, station wagons, light-duty vans, light-duty pickup trucks, and sport utility vehicles that are in good working order and do not leak fluids.
  • Not permittedVehicle repairs on common elements or parking units, or using parking areas to store parts, equipment, or materials.
  • Not permittedParking in designated visitor or accessibility spaces as a resident or unit owner.
  • Visitor parkingThe board may designate visitor spaces, set permitted hours, and require visitor passes. Obtain current instructions from concierge or management.
  • RegistrationThe approved vehicle form warns that unregistered licence plates may result in garage-remote access being deactivated.
  • Rented spaceIf renting a parking space from another resident, provide management with the requested parking-rental information or agreement.
Visitor-parking capacity, hours, and pass procedures may change. Follow current signage and instructions from concierge or property management.

Sources: Schedule XII Rules 10 and 11; Vehicle Registration Form.

12

Switches & Outlets

Some suite wall switches control a receptacle rather than a ceiling light. Other switches may be connected to unused electrical boxes provided for optional lighting that was not installed.

  • Switched outletA wall switch may control one half of a nearby duplex receptacle. The switched half is usually the bottom socket and may be identified by a blue sticker.
  • How to checkPlug a lamp into each half of the receptacle and operate the nearby wall switches. This can help identify which socket is switch-controlled.
  • Kitchen switchesA switch that appears unused may be pre-wired to an unused electrical box intended for optional lighting that was not installed, such as pendant or ceiling lighting.
  • Unit differencesSwitch and receptacle locations can vary by suite layout, and stickers may be missing, moved, or removed. Do not assume every blue-marked or bottom socket is switch-controlled without testing it.
Electrical safety: Do not remove cover plates, open electrical boxes, or alter wiring to investigate a switch. Use a licensed electrician for installation or electrical changes and confirm whether management or corporation approval is required before work begins.

Source: Board-approved building electrical guidance. Original suite configurations and later alterations may differ.

13

Reporting a Concern

Report matters to the party best placed to act. Do not delay an urgent safety, security, water, fire, or access report while waiting for a landlord response.

  1. Emergency or immediate danger: call 911. Notify concierge or management when safe.
  2. Building operations, security, access, noise, parking, pets, waste, or common-element concerns: contact concierge or property management.
  3. Lease terms, rent, unit-owner obligations, or repairs requiring the owner's authority: contact your owner or landlord.
  4. Unresolved condo dispute: document the issue, request a written response, and review the Condominium Authority of Ontario's current dispute-resolution information.

Include These Details

  • Date and time
  • Exact location
  • Clear description
  • Duration or frequency
  • Photos, video, or other evidence where lawful and available
  • Your name, unit number, and contact information
  • Steps already taken
  • Requested action or outcome
About the Condominium Authority Tribunal: current CAO guidance states that tenants cannot file CAT applications. Tenants should work with their landlord where an owner application is required, although tenants may be respondents or may join a case when formally invited. CAT jurisdiction is limited to specified condo disputes.

Official resources: CAT jurisdiction and CAO tenants' guide.
